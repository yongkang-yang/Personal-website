Yongkang Yang

I'm a PhD Candidate at Rotterdam School of Management Eramsus University, in Rotterdam, Netherlands.

At Google I've worked on Glass, Lens Blur, HDR+, VR, Portrait Mode, Portrait Light, and Maps. I did my PhD at UC Berkeley, where I was advised by Jitendra Malik. I've received the PAMI Young Researcher Award.

Research

My research explores how teams evolve and interact in dynamic context, aiming to identify strategies that help teams overcome challenges and capitalize on opportunities to enhance their performance. Additionally, I have broad research interests spanning prosocial behavior, unethical conduct in the workplace, and the potential impact of generative AI on organizations and business practices.

EVER: Exact Volumetric Ellipsoid Rendering for Real-time View Synthesis
Alexander Mai, Peter Hedman, George Kopanas, Dor Verbin, David Futschik, Qiangeng Xu, Falko Kuester, Jonathan T. Barron, Yinda Zhang
arXiv, 2024
project page / arXiv

Raytracing constant-density ellipsoids yields more accurate and flexible radiance fields than splatting Gaussians, and still runs in real-time.

CAT3D: Create Anything in 3D with Multi-View Diffusion Models
Ruiqi Gao*, Aleksander Holynski*, Philipp Henzler, Arthur Brussee, Ricardo Martin Brualla, Pratul P. Srinivasan, Jonathan T. Barron, Ben Poole*
NeurIPS, 2024   (Oral Presentation)
project page / arXiv

A single model built around diffusion and NeRF that does text-to-3D, image-to-3D, and few-view reconstruction, trains in 1 minute, and renders at 60FPS in a browser.
